Explorable Explanations is both a curated index and a design movement, originally articulated by Bret Victor. The core idea: instead of passively reading about a complex system, the reader manipulates it. Sliders, toggles, and simulations let intuition build through experiment rather than through prose alone. The site indexes dozens of explorables across math, biology, economics, social systems, and policy, and links to talks and tools for making your own.

What makes the format work:
- **Direct manipulation collapses the gap** between "I read that" and "I understand that."
- **The reader sets the pace.** Each interaction is a small bet against the model in their head.
- **Failure is cheap.** Tweak, see what breaks, build a richer mental model.

## Why it matters for the project

The NetworkTribes vision involves a lot of interlocking systems — energy, governance, food, money, culture. Static prose flattens them. Three concrete uses:
1. **Component-level explorables.** Each BARK component (the technopark, the regenerative farm, etc.) could ship with a small interactive showing how it changes the local system when added or removed.
2. **Risk and resilience widgets.** Let visitors slide assumptions (drought severity, grid loss duration) and see how the system holds up.
3. **A "make your own" pattern.** Once we have one explorable, the rest become cheaper because we'll have built the design vocabulary.

## Rating

**9 / 10** — A near-essential reference for anyone trying to communicate complexity on the web. Not subtracted further only because the index itself is somewhat dated; the principles are evergreen.
