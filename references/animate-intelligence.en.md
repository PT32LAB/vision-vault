Animate Intelligence is a Substack launched in March 2026 by **Gustaf Palm** — self-described "communitarian, tantrika, burner, regen, occasional degen, nomad and student." Its premise: *"Technology is not outside the living world. It is the living world's newest expression."* The publication rejects the Cartesian split between living and non-living matter and asks what AI development would look like if it were grounded in animist ontologies — reciprocity, place, and entanglement rather than extraction and placelessness. It explicitly refuses both techno-optimism and collapse narratives.

Two cadences: **Pillar Essays** (Thursdays, 2,000–4,000 words) and **Field Notes** (Sundays, 500–1,500 words). The throughline that makes it valuable here is that it is *not* only philosophy — several posts are operational logs from a 2.5-week residency where a dozen people actually tested community-formation methods, plus a data-driven land-selection tool the author built and shipped.

## What it is, in practice

The catalogue splits into three strands:

1. **Animist / philosophical** — the conceptual spine (*What is Animism?*, *The Dragons Have Moved Into the Machines*, *Composting Ourselves*).
2. **Technology-and-ecology** — how machines could be embedded in living systems (*Place-Based AI*, *Solarpunk Worldbuilding*, *Sentience of Machines*, *If Every AI Tool You Use Disappeared Tomorrow*).
3. **Community / land — directly BARK-relevant** — the practical residency log and the land-selection tool (*Tools for Living Together*, *Land Selection Framework*).

## Tools & resources surfaced

The user asked specifically for tools/links. The two community/land posts are where they cluster.

**Built by the author**
- **Land Selection Framework** — live prototype: `land-selection-framework.vercel.app`. Exports to **Jupyter** and **QGIS**; draws on public datasets for climate projections, soil registries, forest-cover trajectories, water-stress models, geopolitical/conflict indices, regenerative-network directories, and land-use history. **Directly reusable for the Coroico siting work.**
- **Mythic Ops** — a personal multi-agent Claude Code orchestration layer with a `/summon` command and named agents (Indra, Heartwood, Forge, Seedbed, Spanda, Alexandria, Canopy, Fathom). Idiosyncratic, but a concrete example of agent-naming-as-design.

**Community-weaving methods tested (from *Tools for Living Together*)**
- Methods: Backcasting · Gift & Role Mapping · The Mirroring Walk · Story Sharing · The Sharing Spectrum · Ideal-Community Axes (15-axis alignment) · Relational Hygiene (tension-clearing).
- Governance frameworks: **Sociocracy** & **Sociocracy 3.0** · **Reinventing Organizations** (Laloux) & the Advice Process · **Core Protocols** (Check-In, Decider).

## Why it matters for the project

1. **Land Selection Framework is an off-the-shelf asset.** Its eight dimensions (climate trajectory, water stress, soil carbon, forest trend, solar potential, conflict proximity, regenerative-network density, population density) are close to what a rigorous semi-autonomous-site analysis for Coroico needs. The "show the axes, let the community judge" stance matches this vault's *total pragmatism* / honest-about-tradeoffs tone. Strong candidate to seed a `tools/` note.
2. **A real tool catalogue for the human layer.** *Tools for Living Together* is effectively a curated index into the community-governance space (Sociocracy 3.0, NVC, restorative circles, ecovillage networks). Several of these — especially Sociocracy 3.0 — are flagged in CLAUDE.md as `tools/` gaps; this post is a ready map of where to look.
3. **Closest living peer to the Low-Tech Magazine framing.** Both treat the *relationship* between technology and the living world as the design variable. *Place-Based AI* and *Solarpunk Worldbuilding* are useful tone/argument references for the technology-sovereignty cluster — energy-bounded, place-bound computation rather than off-grid romance.
4. **Audience overlap.** Readers here already accept reciprocity-with-place and energy-bounded technology as premises — pre-qualified for the BARK framing.

## Limitations

- **Very new and single-author.** Launched March 2026; no track record, no operating community behind it yet. It documents experiments and proposes designs — it does not run a deployed project. Useful as a library and tone reference, not a partner organisation.
- **Place-Based AI is aspirational.** The 150-person, watershed-bounded AI is a thought experiment with no implementation; cite it as vision, not evidence.
- **Mythic Ops / agent-naming content is idiosyncratic** and personal-workflow-specific — interesting framing, low transferable substance.
- **Spiritual/animist register won't travel to every audience** (e.g. investor-facing pages). The operational posts (land selection, community tools, the data-centre cases) are the parts that survive translation to a pragmatic reader.

## Rating

The philosophical essays are a tone match more than a source, but the *Land Selection Framework* and *Tools for Living Together* posts punch well above that: one is a directly reusable siting tool, the other a high-quality index into the community-weaving toolset the vault still lacks.
