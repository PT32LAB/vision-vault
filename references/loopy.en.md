Loopy is a free, browser-based sketchpad for causal-loop diagrams. You draw nodes, connect them with arrows that either reinforce (+) or dampen (-), then hit play and watch the loops oscillate, runaway, or settle. Diagrams can be embedded as live, editable widgets in any web page. No login, no install, no math required.

Why it works:
- **Lowest possible barrier.** A six-year-old can draw a feedback loop in Loopy. Most systems-thinking software demands a course first.
- **Play, not prediction.** Loopy doesn't pretend to model reality — it makes feedback structure visible. That distinction is honest and well-communicated.
- **Embeddable.** Diagrams live in URLs and can be reopened, forked, and dropped into articles.

## Why it matters for the project

Two uses, both immediate:
1. **As a tool we use ourselves.** Almost every BARK component is best understood as a feedback structure (water → soil → forest → microclimate → water). A few embedded Loopy diagrams across the vault would let readers actually grab the system rather than read about it.
2. **As a design reference.** Loopy is also a master class in how to make a "tool that thinks for you" feel inviting — single-page, immediate feedback, generous defaults. Worth studying if we ever build interactive widgets of our own.

## Rating

**9 / 10** — Directly usable both as embeddable tool and as a design reference. The minor minus is that complex multi-loop systems hit Loopy's expressiveness ceiling — beyond a certain depth you need real systems-dynamics tools.
