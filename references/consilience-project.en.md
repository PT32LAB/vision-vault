The Consilience Project is the publishing arm of the Civilization Research Institute. Its central concern is the *quality of public sense-making*: how true facts get assembled into misleading narratives, how bad-faith discourse propagates, and what an information environment compatible with self-governance would actually look like. Articles are long, footnoted, and structurally argumentative — closer to academic essays than blog posts.

Distinctive choices:
- A glossary of foundational terms accompanies the article corpus (treating vocabulary as infrastructure).
- Topics are framed not as "issues" but as *upstream conditions* — fix the information environment and many downstream conflicts dissolve.
- Cross-platform distribution (article, podcast, video) of the same arguments at different depths.

## Why it matters for the project

This is the closest thing to an intellectual peer of the NetworkTribes vision in the English-language space. Useful in three ways:
1. **Argument scaffolding** — when we make the case that current institutions can't reform themselves, their essays are citeable rather than invented from scratch.
2. **Glossary discipline** — naming things matters, and they take it seriously. We should consider a public glossary for terms like "semi-autonomous," "components," "initial spark."
3. **Format depth-tiering** — the article + podcast + short-video pattern is one we could imitate as the vault matures.

## Rating

**9 / 10** — Direct thematic overlap with the project's foundations. Subtract one point for being text-heavy and demanding of the reader; we will need lighter on-ramps in addition.
