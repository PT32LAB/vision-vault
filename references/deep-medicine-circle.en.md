Deep Medicine Circle is a U.S.-based nonprofit organized around five interlocking initiatives — Nourish, Heal, Restore, Story, Learn/Unlearn — staffed by farmers, physicians, herbalists, ecologists, scholars, and youth working together through circle-based decision-making. Their flagship work includes Indigenous land return and decolonized food/medicine programs.

The site itself is the lesson. It uses poetic, aspirational language ("healing the wounds of colonialism through food, medicine, story") paired with photography of land, water, and gathered people. The five-initiative architecture is visually clean enough that someone landing cold can grasp the whole worldview in 30 seconds.

## Why it matters for the project

Two distinct uses:
1. **As a model for our own framing.** The five-initiative structure is structurally similar to the BARK component model. Their site shows how to give each component its own identity without fragmenting the whole.
2. **As a reference for tone.** They write about heavy topics (colonialism, land theft, healing) without doom or guilt-tripping. The reader is welcomed into a project rather than indicted.

Worth studying before we do another pass on the Bolivia landing copy.

## Rating

**8 / 10** — Excellent aesthetic + values alignment. The interaction is more conventional than some others on this list, but the writing and visual identity are top-tier.
