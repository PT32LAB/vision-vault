The Alternative UK publishes "The Daily Alternative," a curated newsletter mapping a global network of *community agency networks* (CANs) — ecovillages, transition towns, mutual-aid neighborhoods, citizen assemblies, regenerative-economy pilots. The editorial frame is "Planet A": instead of waiting for a Plan B from existing institutions, document the parallel polis already being built and treat its participants as protagonists.

Distinctive choices:
- **Solutions-first framing without naïveté.** Coverage assumes the dominant systems are failing; the question is what is being built in the gaps.
- **Daily cadence.** The drip of evidence-of-the-future-arising is itself the argument.
- **Network logic.** Each piece is a node — they consistently connect efforts across geographies rather than treating each as isolated.

## Why it matters for the project

The Alternative is roughly the kind of editorial vehicle the NetworkTribes vault could become if it grew an outward-facing publishing layer. Three uses:
1. **Inbound channel.** Their network already covers the audience we need to reach. Being featured by them — as a Bolivian CAN — is a realistic distribution goal.
2. **Linking partner.** Many of their case studies (transition towns, citizen assemblies, regenerative pilots) are exactly the comparators our vault should be cross-linking to.
3. **Editorial template.** If we ever decide to publish a regular dispatch from BARK, their cadence and framing are a strong starting point.

## Rating

**8 / 10** — Aligned worldview, active publishing operation, and a viable real partnership target. Subtract one point because their visual/UX is functional rather than inspiring — we'd borrow editorial logic, not design.
