Nate Hagens's podcast around the thesis that fossil-fuelled growth is structurally finite and that the coming decades will be a "Great Simplification" — a forced reduction in complexity, throughput, and material expectations. Format is a mix of long expert interviews, short solo "Frankly" reflections, and multi-guest "Reality Roundtables." Topics span energy, money, ecology, geopolitics, AI, psychology, and contemplative practice.

Distinctive moves:
- **No false hope, no doom porn.** Explicit editorial line: "we don't offer easy answers."
- **Systemic framing.** Each episode situates a topic inside the metabolism of the whole civilization rather than as an isolated issue.
- **Personal + structural.** Nearly every episode pairs a societal lens with a "what does this mean for how I live" lens.

## Why it matters for the project

This is the single closest intellectual companion to BARK's foundational thesis (that we are entering a period of involuntary simplification and that semi-autonomous, place-based experiments are part of the answer). Three uses:
1. **Audience overlap.** Listeners of this show are pre-qualified for our message — they already accept the diagnosis.
2. **Tone calibration.** Their refusal of both techno-optimism and collapse-porn is the exact register we want.
3. **Guest pipeline.** The episode list is a who's-who of relevant practitioners and thinkers — useful for sourcing references, partners, and case studies.

## Rating

**9 / 10** — Near-perfect thematic alignment and a model for honest tone. Subtract one point because long-form podcasts are a poor template for a website's communication structure; we borrow tone, not format.
