Reconomy Centre is a physical hub in Totnes, UK — desks, meeting rooms, mentorship, and an annual "Community of Dragons" pitch event — dedicated to incubating ethical and regenerative enterprises in a single small town. It grew out of the Transition Towns movement and operates as connective tissue between social entrepreneurs, ecologically-minded founders, and local funders.

What is interesting:
- **The unit of intervention is one town.** Not a sector, not a movement — a place. That constraint forces the work to be concrete.
- **Physical presence as legitimacy.** A real building with real desks signals that the regenerative economy is a thing you can sit in, not only talk about.
- **Events as deal-flow.** "Community of Dragons" is a Shark-Tank-shaped ritual repurposed for ethical enterprise.

## Why it matters for the project

BARK is, at its heart, an attempt to do for Coroico what Reconomy has been doing for Totnes for a decade — give regenerative enterprise a physical home and a recurring rhythm. Two uses:
1. **Working precedent.** When skeptics ask whether place-based regenerative incubation can sustain itself, Reconomy is a real answer with years of operating history.
2. **Operational reference.** Their event cadence, mentorship model, and tenant mix are templates we can study before designing the technopark's incubation arm.

## Rating

**7 / 10** — Strong precedent and thematic fit, less remarkable as a communication artifact. Useful as a case-study seed; we may eventually want to promote this from `references/` to `case-studies/`.
