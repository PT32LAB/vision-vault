# A Cooperative in the Cloud Forest

**Patient capital. Radical transparency.**

No land has been purchased. No legal entity has been formed. No revenue has been generated. We are publishing this thesis at Phase 0 because we believe the right kind of capital -- patient, aligned, and clear-eyed about risk -- is attracted by honesty, not hype.

---

## The Model: Market connector, not charity

BARK connects Yungas producers to global markets through direct trade, retreat tourism, and technical services. Five integrated components -- techno park, incubator, trading house, retreat center, infrastructure -- share power, labor, and data.

Each component generates independent revenue. Together, they create redundancy: when one stream dips, the others compensate. This is not a single-product bet.

The cooperative structure means every member has voice. Wealth does not buy extra governance. Open-source documentation means the model is forkable -- every replication validates the original.

## Investment Modalities (Post-Entity Formation)

Capital cannot be accepted until a legal cooperative is formed. These are the structures we are designing for:

1. **Cooperative Shares** -- Membership under Bolivia's Ley 356. One member, one vote. Foreign nationals welcome.
2. **Component Funding** -- Fund a solar micro-grid, coffee line, or fab lab. Your capital becomes a named, physical thing.
3. **In-Kind Contributions** -- Ship panels, tools, or contribute engineering designs. Valued at fair market, converted to shares.
4. **Residency Investment** -- Buy-in for community membership plus living space. Land remains collectively owned.
5. **Working Contributions** -- Earn shares through labor -- construction, agriculture, teaching, maintenance. Sweat equity with caps.

## What's Different: Not a startup. Not a charity.

**Cooperative structure.** Bolivia's Ley 356 allows foreign membership with full voice and vote. Governance cannot be purchased -- one member, one vote. See [[comparative-models]].

**Open source.** Every schematic, every governance decision, every agricultural trial published. The model survives its founders and resists capture.

**Government alignment.** Bolivia actively seeks rural electrification, cooperative formation, and agroforestry investment. BARK aligns with existing national programs, not against them.
