# Region, Not Enclave

BARK is being built in the Yungas to increase local capability, not to withdraw from the region that makes it possible.

The project only has legitimacy if it contributes to the surrounding territory: producers, suppliers, skills, infrastructure, and new forms of regional economic life.

![Tocana landscape, Yungas valley below Coroico](/images/yungas/tocana-landscape.jpg)

## What BARK Means for This Place

**Capability, not enclosure.**

1. **Local suppliers** -- Construction materials, food, transport, services. BARK buys locally first. Every purchase is a vote for the regional economy.
2. **Jobs** -- Local hiring for construction, agriculture, hospitality, maintenance. BARK is a payroll before it is a product.
3. **Market access** -- The [[trading|Trading House]] buys local surplus at fair prices and connects Yungas producers to international buyers. Growers keep their land and their margin.
4. **Producer partnerships** -- Direct relationships with coffee, coca, citrus, and cacao growers. Shared processing, shared logistics, shared upside.
5. **Infrastructure** -- Solar arrays, water systems, mesh communications -- built to benefit the surrounding area, not fence it off.
6. **Skills** -- Training programs, Carmen Pampa University partnership, tech transfer. Knowledge that stays in the valley.
7. **Ventures** -- The [[incubator|Incubator]] launches businesses rooted in local needs -- processing, logistics, services the region actually lacks.

## Why Here: Coroico and the Yungas

1,750 meters. Year-round growing season. Three hours from La Paz via a single road that doubles as lifeline and bottleneck. Extraordinary biodiversity across altitude bands. An existing cooperative tradition through CENAPROC and the broader Yungas agricultural network. Carmen Pampa University within reach for research and training. The infrastructure of mutual aid already exists here -- BARK plugs into it, not over it.

## Regional Lift Model

**How the ecosystem connects.**

BARK is one node in a regional network. Value flows outward -- to producers, cooperatives, the university, local suppliers, and eventually to new ventures and markets that did not exist before.

- **Inner ring (direct partners):** Farmers, Cooperatives, University, Suppliers, Ventures
- **Outer ring (downstream reach):** Markets, Visitors, Talent

## What BARK Is Not

**No enclaves. No exceptions.**

- **Not a ZEDE** -- No special economic zone. No separate jurisdiction. Bolivian law applies fully.
- **Not an expat bunker** -- The cooperative requires Bolivian leadership by law (Ley 356 Art. 65). This is by design.
- **Not extractive** -- We buy local, process local, sell for local benefit. Value stays in the Yungas.
- **Not replacing livelihoods** -- Market connector, not competitor. Farmers gain a buyer, not a rival.

## Partnership Paths

**How the relationship works.**

### Producers

Sell to the Trading House at transparent, fair-market prices. Keep your land, keep your margin.

### Cooperatives

Shared processing equipment, shared market access, shared logistics.

### Schools & University

Internships, field research, training programs with Carmen Pampa. Student projects with real outcomes.

### Municipal & Civic Bodies

Alignment with Bolivia's $325M solar program, cooperative legal framework, and local development goals.

### Technical Allies

Engineers, agronomists, builders, researchers. Contribute expertise, access shared [[infrastructure]].

### Local Institutions

Health posts, transport operators, market associations. BARK integrates with what already works.

## Risks We Acknowledge

**Honest about the hard parts.**

- **Road access** -- the La Paz-Coroico highway closes during landslides. Supply chain resilience is not optional.
- **Healthcare** -- limited local facilities. Nearest hospital is in La Paz, three hours away.
- **Political instability** -- Bolivia has real political cycles. The 2024 crisis disrupted the entire country.
- **Bureaucracy** -- permits, inspections, legal processes move on their own timeline. Patience is a structural requirement.
- **Economic crisis** -- currency pressure, inflation, reserve depletion. We plan for this, not around it.
- **Local legitimacy must be earned** -- no amount of investment substitutes for trust. That takes presence, time, and follow-through.

We have researched this honestly. See [[risks|full risk assessment]].
