# Soil and Voltage. A Community That Tends Itself.

**Coroico, Bolivia -- 1,750 m**

BARK -- the **Bolivian Ark** -- is a community learning to provision itself in the Yungas cloud forest. Five organs, one body. All of it published. All of it forkable. The plans belong to anyone willing to use them.

![Yungas valley](/images/yungas/valley.jpg)

## What This Is

Not a bunker. Not a resort. A community that grows its own food, generates its own power, fixes its own equipment, and governs itself.

Energy, food, manufacturing, commerce, governance -- five systems designed to keep each other running. The solar array powers the fabrication lab that maintains the irrigation pump that waters the food forest that feeds the people who vote on the budget. Remove one loop and the others compensate. **The entire design is open-source, because a model that cannot be replicated dies with its founders.**

![Cloud forest slopes of the Yungas](/images/yungas/yungas-landscape.jpg)

## The Thesis

**The supply chain is the civilization.**

Every generation assumes the grid will hold. Ours watches semiconductor shortages idle factories in six weeks, currency crises empty shelves overnight, and a single blocked canal rearrange global trade for months. The systems we depend on were optimized for efficiency, not for surviving their own fragility.

> "A solar installation takes months. A food forest takes years to fruit. Trust between strangers who share a water source compounds slowly and cannot be purchased."

## Location: Coroico, Yungas

The road from La Paz drops 3,400 meters in three hours. Altiplano dust thins. Bromeliads appear on the rock cuts. The air goes wet and warm, smelling of soil and rotting fruit. You have crossed into the Yungas -- a climate band where nothing freezes, nothing scorches, and food grows twelve months without pause. The Inca terraced these slopes a thousand years ago. The knowledge is in the landscape itself.

## Five Components, One System

### 01 -- [[technopark|Techno Park]]

A fabrication lab where broken water pumps get fixed, circuit boards get milled, and the tools the supply chain abandoned get redesigned in the open.

### 02 -- [[incubator|Incubator]]

Ventures that prove themselves in Coroico before they scale anywhere else. Built around local needs and local supply chains.

### 03 -- [[trading|Trading House]]

Yungas coffee and cacao moving through channels we built -- not middlemen who have never tasted the fruit wet off the branch.

### 04 -- [[retreat|Retreat Center]]

Biometric-monitored contemplative practice. Aymara healers and biosensors in the same room, learning from each other.

### 05 -- [[infrastructure|Infrastructure]]

Solar panels, spring-fed cisterns, vertical growing walls, mesh radios. The foundation that keeps everything else running.
