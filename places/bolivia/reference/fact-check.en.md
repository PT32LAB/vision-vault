## Key Issues Found

The fact-check process identified several systemic issues across the site:

### 1. Present-tense language for things that do not exist yet

The Vision page says "BARK hires locally, buys locally" while the homepage CTA says "first cohort is forming." The Retreat Center describes guest experiences; the Incubator describes mentorship from experienced founders. None of these exist yet. BARK has no legal entity, no land, and no revenue. The Roadmap and Investors pages disclose this clearly, but other pages use present tense that could mislead casual readers.

*Affects: Homepage, Vision, Incubator, Retreat, Participate*

### 2. Economic data that may be stale or volatile

Bolivia's economy is changing rapidly. Foreign reserves (~$100M), inflation (20%+), credit rating (CCC-), and the dollar gap (70-115%) are all 2024-2025 snapshots that may be outdated by the time you read this. Coffee FOB pricing ($4.60/lb) and cost-of-living figures ($500-800/month) are also time-sensitive. The Gingery book set price ($50) is outdated -- current price is $80-120.

*Affects: Resilience, Economy, Investors, Technopark*

### 3. Scenario scoring methodology lacks disclosed weights

The Scenarios page presents a 10-factor weighting model across 6 crisis scenarios with precise numerical scores (e.g., Bolivia 9.0 for Trust Crisis). The factors, weights, and scoring criteria are not disclosed, making the rankings impossible to verify or replicate. Self-generated scores are presented alongside externally sourced data, creating false equivalence between verified facts and internal ratings.

*Affects: Scenarios*

### 4. WHO ranking from 2000 presented without date caveat

The Resilience page cites Bolivia's WHO healthcare ranking as 126th of 190. This ranking is from the year 2000 and has not been updated by WHO since. Bolivia's healthcare system has changed significantly in 26 years.

*Affects: Resilience*

### 5. Contradictions between pages

- Family farms food share now consistently "80-90%" across pages (previously ~98% vs 88%)
- Portfolio break-even now consistently "3-4 years" across Economy and Investors pages (previously 12-18 months vs 3-4 years)
- RepRap self-replication "73%" (table) vs "50-70%" (text) on the same Technopark page
- Fab Lab count "1,500+" (Technopark) vs "2,500+" (Network)
- Bolivia homicide rate "4.3/100K" (Risks) vs "4.4/100K" (FAQ)
- "5,000+ medicinal plant species" in Yungas (Technopark, Incubator) vs "3,000+ plant species" total (Location) -- 5,000 medicinal likely conflates national with regional figure

### 6. Optimism bias in financial projections

All financial projections assume best-case operating conditions: skilled staff available, market access established, equipment operational, supply chains working. No page addresses the compound probability that all five components must succeed simultaneously. Year 1 profit projections of $43K-$129K on $65K-$158K investment imply 66-82% first-year returns, which is unusual for agricultural processing startups.

*Affects: Technopark, Economy, Investors*

## Methodology

Claims on this site are verified against primary sources: government databases, academic papers, official reports, legal texts, and recognized international organizations. We do not rely on social media, anonymous forums, or undated blog posts as primary evidence.

### Confidence Levels -- Facts

- **Verified** -- Confirmed against a primary source (government data, legal text, direct measurement)
- **Supported** -- Confirmed against a credible secondary source (reputable journalism, institutional reports)
- **Qualified** -- True with caveats -- the claim is broadly correct but depends on methodology, timeframe, or definition
- **Unverified** -- Stated on the site but we could not confirm it against any independent source

### Confidence Levels -- Arguments

- **Sound** -- Logically valid argument -- premises are supported and the conclusion follows
- **Plausible** -- Reasonable but debatable -- the argument holds under stated assumptions but is not proven
- **Weak** -- Logical gaps or unsupported leaps -- the reasoning has identifiable problems
- **Speculative** -- No evidence base -- pure projection or aspirational framing presented as likely

### Process

1. **Automated pass** -- every link tested, every statistic cross-referenced against its cited source
2. **Manual fact-check** -- each factual claim traced to its primary source and confidence level assigned
3. **Argument audit** -- every logical argument assessed for soundness, with premises checked against evidence
4. **Cross-page consistency check** -- contradictions, stale data, and implicit claims identified across all 27 pages

All research documents are public: [GitHub research folder](https://github.com/PT32LAB/PT32LAB.github.io/tree/main/research).

## Known Limitations

- **Coroico-specific crime statistics.** Bolivia publishes national and departmental data, but no public municipal-level crime data exists for Nor Yungas province.
- **Current internet speeds in Coroico.** No public speed-test monitoring data for the area. Starlink availability is confirmed; actual user experience is anecdotal.
- **Exact land prices.** Bolivia has no public real-estate registry with transaction prices for the Coroico area.
- **Hospital staffing levels.** Coroico has a hospital, but staffing data is not published online.
- **2024 census detailed results.** Bolivia's most recent detailed census data at the municipal level dates from 2012.
- **Intentional community failure rate.** Widely cited ("most fail within five years") but the specific primary study is not easily traceable.
- **IDB $4.5B development package.** Cited on Economy, Investors, and Network pages but no primary IDB source has been independently confirmed.
- **Post-2025 political events.** Claims about Bolivia's August 2025 election, US-Bolivia restored relations, and USAID closure are based on evolving political situations and may be stale.
