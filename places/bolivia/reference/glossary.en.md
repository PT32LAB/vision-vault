# Terms & Concepts

These terms appear throughout the BARK project. Definitions are provided for clarity and cultural context.

![Panoramic view of the Yungas cloud forest landscape](/images/yungas/yungas-landscape.jpg)

---

**Achachilas** -- Mountain spirits in Aymara cosmology.

**AFCOOP** -- Autoridad de Fiscalizacion y Control de Cooperativas. Bolivia's cooperative regulator.

**ANMI** -- Area Natural de Manejo Integrado. Managed conservation area designation in Bolivia.

**Appropriate technology** -- Technology that is suitable for local conditions -- maintainable, repairable, and useful by the community that uses it.

**Ayllu** -- Pre-colonial Andean governance model based on collective agriculture, kinship networks, and reciprocal obligations. Constitutionally recognized in Bolivia since 2009. See [[comparative-models]].

**Ayni** -- Aymara/Quechua concept of reciprocal labor exchange. I help you today, you help me tomorrow.

**BARK** -- Bolivian Ark. The name of this project.

**Cooperative (Cooperativa)** -- Legal organizational form in Bolivia under Ley 356 (2013). Minimum 10 members, one-member-one-vote. See [[comparative-models]].

**DAO** -- Decentralized Autonomous Organization. Token-based governance using blockchain.

**GVCS** -- Global Village Construction Set. Open Source Ecology's suite of 50 open-source machines for civilization.

**Ley 356** -- Bolivia's 2013 General Cooperative Law governing all cooperative organizations.

**Mandar obedeciendo** -- "Lead by obeying." Zapatista governance principle where leaders execute the assembly's decisions.

**Minka (Mink'a)** -- Communal labor for community benefit. Everyone contributes work to shared projects.

**NetworkTribes** -- The knowledge framework designed by Oleg. Maps ideas across multiple dimensions for exploration. See [[knowledge-dimensions]].

**Pachamama** -- Earth Mother in Andean cosmology. Central to Aymara and Quechua spiritual practice.

**RepRap** -- Replicating Rapid Prototyper. Open-source 3D printer project designed to print its own components.

**Saya** -- Traditional Afro-Bolivian music and dance form, central to the cultural identity of Yungas communities.

**Semi-autonomous** -- BARK's operating philosophy: capable of independent operation when needed, normally integrated with regional systems.

**Technoshamanism** -- BARK's term for integrating technology-assisted practices (biometric monitoring, data analysis) with traditional healing and contemplative practices.

**Yungas** -- Transitional ecological zone on the eastern slopes of the Andes. Subtropical montane cloud forest between the altiplano and the Amazon basin.
