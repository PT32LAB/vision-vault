# Build Real Systems in the Yungas

BARK is a place to build infrastructure, tools, and ventures under real constraints in the Bolivian Yungas. Energy, water, fabrication, communications, local production -- designed at 1,750m, documented for replication.

![Road carved into Yungas mountainside](/images/yungas/death-road.jpg)

---

## Why Builders Matter Here

This is not abstract design work.

**Isolation creates design pressure.** When resupply takes days and the nearest city is hours away, every system must be robust, repairable, and locally sourceable.

**Repair matters more than novelty.** The Yungas does not need bleeding-edge prototypes. It needs things that work when the road washes out.

**Regional problems create product opportunities.** Solutions built for these constraints -- energy, water, comms, processing -- have markets across rural Latin America.

**Open-source infrastructure has regional value.** Documented, forkable systems can be replicated by other communities facing similar conditions.

## What Gets Built: Six Systems, One Site

### 01 -- Energy

- **Problem:** Unreliable grid, high altitude UV, wet-season cloud cover.
- **Build:** Solar arrays, micro-hydro turbines, battery storage, grid tie-in.
- **Why:** Energy independence is the foundation -- nothing else works without it.

### 02 -- Water

- **Problem:** No municipal treatment, seasonal variation, steep terrain runoff.
- **Build:** Spring capture, ceramic and biosand filtration, greywater recycling, rainwater harvesting.
- **Why:** 1,300mm/yr of rainfall is an asset if you can capture, clean, and store it.

### 03 -- Communications

- **Problem:** No fiber, no cell towers in many valleys, single point of failure.
- **Build:** Starlink backhaul, LoRa mesh for site telemetry, HF radio backup, local compute servers.
- **Why:** Connectivity enables remote work, telemedicine, and market access.

### 04 -- Fabrication

- **Problem:** Replacement parts take weeks. Nearest industrial supplier is La Paz.
- **Build:** 3D printing, CNC routing, Gingery-style foundry, RepRap self-replication.
- **Why:** Make the tools that make the parts. Repair matters more than novelty.

### 05 -- Agro-Processing

- **Problem:** Raw commodities leave the region at commodity prices.
- **Build:** Coffee wet mill, cacao fermentation line, solar dehydrators, essential oil distillation.
- **Why:** Value-add at origin captures margin that currently leaks to intermediaries.

See also: [[trading-house]]

### 06 -- Local Manufacturing

- **Problem:** Consumer goods arrive expensive, fragile, and ill-suited to conditions.
- **Build:** Earthbag and ferrocement construction, greenhouse structures, aquaponics, recycled-plastic products.
- **Why:** Regional manufacturing reduces dependency and creates local employment.

## Build Pipeline: From Local Need to Replicable System

| Step | Title | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 01 | Identify local need | Talk to residents, observe failures, map what breaks and what is missing. |
| 02 | Prototype | Build a minimum viable version using available materials and tools. |
| 03 | Test on site | Deploy under real conditions -- altitude, humidity, road access, power variability. |
| 04 | Refine | Fix what failed. Simplify what was over-engineered. Reduce cost and complexity. |
| 05 | Document | Publish plans, BOMs, test data, and failure reports under open licenses. |
| 06 | Replicate or spin into venture | Scale to other sites, or build a business around the proven solution. |

## Technopark vs. Infrastructure

### [[technopark|Technopark]]

Invention, prototyping, tools, repair, and venture support. The technopark is where new solutions are designed, tested, and refined before deployment.

- Fabrication workshops (3D printing, CNC, foundry)
- Electronics and embedded systems lab
- Product design and testing facilities
- Incubation space for local ventures

### [[infrastructure|Infrastructure]]

Energy, water, habitat, communications, and the operational backbone. Infrastructure is what keeps the site running day to day, season to season.

- Solar + micro-hydro energy systems
- Water capture, filtration, and recycling
- Communications backbone (Starlink, LoRa, HF)
- Buildings, roads, and site maintenance

## Open Source Layer

Everything documented. Everything published.

Plans, bills of materials, test results, failure reports -- all published under open licenses. Fork the designs. Adapt them to your site. Send improvements back.

The stack builds on what already exists: the Global Village Construction Set, RepRap self-replicating fabrication, Precious Plastic recycling machines. We are not inventing from zero. We are integrating, testing, and documenting at a specific site with specific constraints.

Documentation is not an afterthought. It is the primary output. A system that works but cannot be replicated is a hobby project, not infrastructure.

## Risks and Realities

What you should know before committing:

**Road access.** A single road connects to La Paz. Landslides close it regularly in wet season (Dec-Mar).

**Healthcare limits.** Basic clinics exist in Coroico. Serious medical care requires evacuation to La Paz.

**Material scarcity.** Specialty components, industrial materials, and electronics must be sourced from La Paz or imported.

**Climate and terrain.** 1,750m altitude, high humidity, steep slopes. Rusts uncoated steel in weeks. Limits construction methods.

**Local fit matters.** Solutions must work for the people already here. Imported assumptions fail. Listen first.
