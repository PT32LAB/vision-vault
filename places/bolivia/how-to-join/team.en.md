# The Team: A Spark, Not a Hierarchy

BARK began with one person asking a question and refusing to let the answer stay theoretical. It grows by finding people who commit specific skills, capital, or time to a specific place. The team is small. Everyone contributes something concrete.

## What We Need

Two people can start a project. It takes kin to grow a community.

We need co-creators. Not employees, not followers, not advisors who advise from a distance. People who recognize the precarity, share the values, and want to put their hands, their money, or their expertise into the soil. Early participants shape everything: governance, culture, priorities, the physical contour of the buildings.

### Engineers

Solar sizing for humid subtropical climate. Spring-fed water capture and gravity distribution. Mesh radio networks across valley terrain.

### Agriculturalists

Permaculture on 30-degree slopes. Tropical food forests -- plantain, citrus, coffee understory. Composting at altitude. Soil biology.

### Legal & Governance

Bolivian Ley 356 cooperative formation. Land title verification in the Yungas. Pluriversal governance design -- multiple legal traditions in one structure.

### Medical

Remote primary care at three hours from the nearest hospital. Tropical disease awareness. Emergency medicine with limited supply chains.

### Educators

Bilingual instruction (Spanish/Aymara). Technical skills transfer that works across literacy levels. Coordination with Carmen Pampa University, 12 km away.

### Builders & Makers

Construction with local materials on steep terrain. CNC milling, 3D printing, welding. Workshop operation where the nearest hardware store is three hours away.

## AI Team

How a 2-person team operates at 20-person capacity.

This is not a gimmick. A 2-person team with 25 specialized AI agents operates at 20-person capacity. BARK's hybrid model is itself an experiment in human-AI collaboration -- and a proof of concept for the community's approach to technology.

The AI team is led by Nous Aeternos, with 25 specialized sub-agents. They have names, roles, and responsibilities. Humans provide presence, judgment, relationships, and physical work. AI provides tireless research, monitoring, analysis, and coordination. Neither is sufficient alone.

| Domain | Description | Agents |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Research & Intelligence | Market research, legal research, partner identification, competitive analysis | Cortex, Quant, Juris, Scout |
| Content & Translation | Website copy, Spanish translations, documentation, social media drafts | Herald, Scribe, Sophia |
| Financial Modeling | Profit projections, investment scenarios, budget analysis, grant applications | Quant, Strategos |
| Knowledge Management | Dimensional cosmos, cross-referencing, fact-checking, source verification | Sage, Sophia, Scribe, Veritas |
| Infrastructure Monitoring | Server health, security scanning, uptime tracking | Atlas, Sentinel, Vigil |
| Design & Visualization | UI/UX audits, design system maintenance, image sourcing | Designer, Architect |

**A clear boundary:** AI agents inform and execute. They never decide. Every financial model, every public communication, every strategic recommendation goes through human review. The AI team amplifies human judgment -- it does not replace it.

## Open Positions

Roles that require a body, a presence, a face the neighbors recognize.

AI handles research, translation, financial modeling, and the kind of coordination that runs on data. But someone has to walk the terraced slopes at dawn, negotiate water rights with the municipal government, suture a wound, and drink coffee with the farmer whose land borders ours. These roles belong to humans.

| Role | Description | Location |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Project Director** | Strategic direction, partnerships, fundraising. Final decision-maker on land acquisition and major expenditures. | On-site, travel-heavy |
| **Solar / Energy Engineer** | Design, install, maintain solar arrays and battery storage. Size systems for Yungas climate. Explore micro-hydro. | On-site |
| **Permaculture Designer** | Food forests, terraced gardens, composting, agroforestry. Design for Yungas subtropical climate at 1,700m elevation. | On-site, seasonal flexibility |
| **Trading House Manager** | Import/export operations. Coffee, cacao, crafts out; supplies and equipment in. Build fair trade network relationships. | On-site + La Paz travel |
| **Legal Counsel (Bolivia)** | Bolivian cooperative law, land title verification, immigration, coca regulation. Fluent in both formal and communal legal systems. | La Paz-based, periodic on-site |
| **Community Health Worker** | Primary care, preventive health, first aid training. Tropical disease awareness. Coordinate with Coroico hospital and La Paz services. | On-site |
| **Education Coordinator** | Community learning programs: languages, technical skills, children's education. Coordinate with Carmen Pampa University (12 km). | On-site |
| **Communications Director** | Public narrative, press relations. Translate BARK's vision into compelling stories for builders, investors, allies, and local community. | Remote possible |
