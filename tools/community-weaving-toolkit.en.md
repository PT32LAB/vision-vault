This note is a classified inventory of the toolkit surfaced in the *Tools for Living Together* essay ([[animate-intelligence]]) — the relational and governance methods a dozen people field-tested over a 2.5-week residency to "weave" a community **before** selecting land. The essay's thesis: *trust comes before clarity, and most groups try it the other way around.* Where the [[land-selection-framework]] answers *where to settle*, this toolkit answers *whether the group can hold together at all.*

The point of the inventory is **analysis and classification** — every link from the source, sorted by what it is, what cluster it serves, and whether it deserves its own vault note later. Standalone notes already exist for **Sociocracy 3.0** (see [[sociocracy-3-0]]) and the **Land Selection Framework**.

## 1. In-house methods (no external link — practices the residency ran)

These were exercises the group invented or adapted; they have no canonical website. All sit in **human-potential / governance**.

| Method | What it is | Note-worthy? |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Backcasting** | Work backward from a chosen future settlement date to name tipping points and immediate next actions. | Maybe — generic foresight tool, low priority. |
| **Creative Project Presentations** | 4-minute personal pitches with keyword capture to find overlapping interests. | No — exercise, not a tool. |
| **Nature & Object Reflection** | Members bring found objects representing community qualities they want. | No. |
| **Gift & Role Mapping** | Identify the archetypal roles a community needs *before* assigning people. | No. |
| **The Mirroring Walk** | One-on-one walk where each reflects back the other's unseen gifts. | No. |
| **Story Sharing** | Vulnerable surfacing of the narratives members hold about each other. | No. |
| **The Sharing Spectrum** | Map each person's private↔collective preference across money, labour, time, emotional space. | Maybe — useful diagnostic for BARK membership design. |
| **Ideal-Community Axes** | Overlay 15-axis preference profiles to surface real disagreements early. | Maybe — pairs with Sharing Spectrum. |
| **Relational Hygiene** | A standing pathway to clear tension before it turns destructive. | Maybe — overlaps NVC / Restorative Circles. |
| **Five Elements of Community** (Michel Bachmann) | Fire (attraction), the one-to-one web, rhythms, circles of commitment, the arrival/departure spiral. | Maybe — a tidy framing concept (`vision/`). |

## 3. Dialogue, communication & conflict practices

The "can we stand each other / repair harm" layer. Cluster: **human-potential** (with governance overlap).

| Practice | Link | What it is | Standalone note? |
|----------|------|-----------|------------------|

## Why it matters for the project

A semi-autonomous Coroico community will live or die on the human layer long before infrastructure matters. This inventory turns one essay into a **map of the field**: where to find decision-making mechanics (§2), conflict repair (§3), curricula and possible partners (§4), and living precedents to learn from (§5). It complements the technical/siting work in [[land-selection-framework]] and the decision mechanics in [[sociocracy-3-0]].

## Limitations / tradeoffs

- **Breadth over depth.** This catalogs ~35 items at one line each; none is a substitute for the standalone notes above.
- **Single-source.** Everything here comes from one essay's curation — it reflects that author's lineage (ZEGG/Tamera, Teal, AR/Circling), which skews European-intentional-community and may under-represent Indigenous, Latin-American, and faith-based community-governance traditions more relevant to Coroico.
- **Practices vary wildly in cost and intensity.** Forum and Circling need trained facilitation and emotional safety; consent decisions and the Advice Process are comparatively lightweight. Don't treat the list as flat.
